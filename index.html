🎓 M.Tech Thesis

Exploring the Impact of Land Use/ Land Cover Factors on Surface Runoff through a ML model

Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur (IITK)

IIT Kanpur | 2022 – 2024

Supervisor: Dr. Rajesh Srivastava

🔍 Overview

This research investigates the impact of Land Use/Land Cover (LULC) changes and climatic factors on surface runoff using geospatial analysis and machine learning techniques. The study focuses on the Kanpur region, analyzing long-term environmental changes and their influence on hydrological processes.

📊 Key Work
  • Processed and classified 19 years (2002–2020) of Landsat satellite data using ArcGIS Pro.
  • Performed LULC classification into Urban, Agriculture, Forest, and Water Body using supervised learning.
  • Developed a Random Forest machine learning model to predict surface runoff.
  • Integrated multiple parameters including rainfall, temperature, humidity, wind speed, and previous-day rainfall.
  • Applied spatial interpolation techniques to align climatic and runoff datasets.
📈 Key Findings
  • Urban areas and water bodies showed continuous growth, while agricultural and forest areas declined over time.
  • Rainfall, temperature, relative humidity, and wind speed were primary drivers of surface runoff.
  • LULC factors influenced runoff but had comparatively lower impact than climatic variables.
  • Agricultural regions showed higher runoff in high rainfall conditions due to nitrogen contribution.
  • Achieved model accuracy of ~84% (R² score) using Random Forest.
🛠️ Tools & Technologies

ArcGIS Pro | Python | Machine Learning (Random Forest) | Remote Sensing | Spatial Analysis

✅ Conclusion

The study highlights the combined impact of climatic factors and land use changes on surface runoff, with rainfall and environmental conditions playing a dominant role. It demonstrates the integration of geospatial analysis with machine learning for improved water resource planning.

Design and Analysis of G+11 Residential Building

Turkish Online Journal of Qualitative Inquiry (TOJQI)
Oct 2021 ▼
Read Full Paper

🔍 Overview

This study focuses on the seismic analysis and design of a G+11 reinforced concrete residential building located in a seismic Zone-III region. The structure was analyzed using the Equivalent Static Method in STAAD.Pro to ensure structural safety and performance under earthquake loading.

📊 Key Contributions

  • Performed seismic analysis using Equivalent Static Method considering critical load combinations as per IS 1893:2016.
  • Evaluated structural response parameters including displacement, deflection, and bending moment across all floors.
  • Designed and analyzed the structure using STAAD.Pro, ensuring compliance with Indian Standard codes for stability.

✅ Conclusion

The analysis confirmed that all structural responses were within permissible limits, proving the effectiveness of the Equivalent Static Method for mid-rise seismic-resistant buildings.

A Study on Recycled Lightweight Aggregate Concrete

Journal of Building Pathology and Rehabilitation
Feb 2022 ▼
Read Full Paper

🔍 Overview

This research explores the use of recycled concrete aggregates (RCA) from construction waste as a sustainable alternative to natural aggregates, evaluating mechanical performance and environmental impact.

📊 Key Contributions

  • Investigated RCA impact on concrete properties through systematic laboratory test series.
  • Evaluated compressive and flexural strength at 7, 14, and 28-day curing intervals.
  • Assessed workability and performance characteristics compared to natural aggregate concrete.

✅ Conclusion

The findings highlight that a 30% replacement of natural aggregates with recycled ones achieves optimal strength, providing a practical solution for waste reduction in construction.
